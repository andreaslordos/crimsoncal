Description

What is known as the Renaissance was a period of intense cultural transformation, a history and legacy we have inherited. During this time, artists reached an unprecedented stature and created some of the most powerful works of art in Europe. From the construction of the liberal arts to cultural encounters and territorial expansion, we live with the traditions of the Renaissance and its repercussions. This course traces an artistic narrative that moves from Giotto to Caravaggio, uncovering their poignant inquiries into nature, beauty and Antiquity within the shifting political and religious terrains of their times. We will look closely at the art of Masaccio, Donatello, Botticelli, Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Titian and Raphael and their contemporaries. We will be attentive to meaning and interpretation, both the ways artists made meaning and the ways we find it in the Italian Renaissance, in its beauty and in its power.
